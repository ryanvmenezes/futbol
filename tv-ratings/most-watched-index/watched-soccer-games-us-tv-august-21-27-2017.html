Most-watched soccer games on US TV for August 21-27, 2017

Collin Werner September 1, 2017 TV Ratings 6 Comments

Last week saw two Premier League matches break the one million viewer mark. Saturday afternoon’s match between Manchester United and Leicester City drew 1.137 million viewers across NBC and Universo, up 38% from the same window last week and up 15% from the opening weekend. Then on Sunday, Liverpool’s 4-0 destruction of Arsenal drew 1.073 million viewers on NBCSN and Telemundo, up 4% from the opening weekend match which featured Chicharito’s West Ham United debut.

Bundesliga match week two ratings were up 56% from last season. Viewership peaked on Saturday when both Bayern Munich and Borussia Dortmund were featured on FOX Sports 1. Through the first two weeks of the season, eight of the 11 Bundesliga matches available to cable subscribers have been on FOX Sports 1 with those matches averaging 104,750 viewers with Spanish-language viewership included.

MLS saw a slight increase in viewership for Heineken Rivalry Week broadcasts with the weekends four nationally-televised matches averaging 250,250 viewers on ESPN and FOX Sports 1, up just 7% from the 234,500 average cable viewers for the season before this weekend. However, when comparing this weekend’s numbers to the six other matches broadcast in August, viewership was up 27%.
