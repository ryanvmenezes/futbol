Most-watched soccer games on US TV for February 2-27, 2018

Collin Werner March 5, 2018 TV Ratings 7 Comments

The month of February saw six matches break the 1 million viewer mark with 3 of them coming on February 10. Manchester City and Leicester City kicked things off by drawing a total of 1.245 million viewers on NBC and Universo, which ranked as the fourth most-watched soccer match of 2018. Later that night Univision’s Liga MX doubleheader gave us the two most-watched soccer matches of 2018 thus far as Tigres hosted Club América before an audience of 1.479 million and was followed by Chivas and Santos Laguna playing before 1.36 million American TV viewers.

Manchester City’s 3-0 dismantling of Arsenal in the Carabao Cup Final drew 672,000 viewers on ESPN, marking the tournaments most-watched final in recent years with a 197% increase in viewership from the 2017 Final on beIN Sports.

The February 20 Champions League Round of 16 meeting between Chelsea and Barcelona provided FOX Sports 1 with its largest audience for a Champions League match this season at 411,000 viewers. Real Madrid’s meeting with Paris Saint-Germain, meanwhile, averaged more than 300,000 viewers.
